About SHPE

The Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE) was founded in Los Angeles, California, in 1974 by a group of engineers employed by the city of Los Angeles. Their objective was to form a national organization of professional engineers to serve as role models in the Hispanic community. The concept of Networking was the key basis for the organization. SHPE quickly established two student chapters to begin the network that would grow to encompass the nation as well as reach countries outside the United States. Today, SHPE enjoys a strong but independent network of professional and student chapters throughout the nation.  SHPE changes lives by empowering the Hispanic community to realize its fullest potential, and to impact the world through STEM awareness, access, support and development. 

In the fall 1987 semester, hermano Carlos Menjivar of Phi Iota Alpha Fraternity, Inc., the Oldest Latino Fraternity in Existence, found a need for a Latino representation in the engineering field at Stony Brook University. The inception of the idea of such an organization came from hermanos Carlos Menjivar and Luis A. Gomez. Their vision was that this organization would unite, support, and empower Latinos in the engineering field. With the support of the Engineering department, their first meeting was a huge success. At this time, the organization was known as The Society of Hispanic Engineers (S.H.E) and by a unanimous vote, the first Executive Board was elected. This organization would move forward with efforts based on field trips and hosting professional guest speakers so that students have the opportunity to network and build relations with individuals in their prospective discipline. Moreover, the organization would emphasize on increasing the number of Hispanic engineering students on campus and most importantly, provide proper guidance that would allow these students to flourish and receive his or her degree(s).

  In the fall 1996 semester, the society decided to change their name from The Society of Hispanic Engineers (S.H.E.) at Stony Brook University to the Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (S.H.P.E) at Stony Brook University in hope of giving the organization a rebirth at the university.  Since then, we have led a continuously growing organization that enriches the diversity of the Stony Brook campus and contributes to the growing technical talent. Our chapter currently has over 80 registered members, many of which come from Hispanic and other diverse backgrounds. Of our registered members, over 20 are actively involved in our chapter, which makes us one of the largest engineering societies at Stony Brook. SHPE dedicates its mission by spreading awareness of the subjects of Science, Technology, Engineering and Mathematics (S.T.E.M) in our Hispanic communities.

Join SHPE

Getting involved with Stony Brook SHPE is simple! Our general body meetings occur every week on
 Mondays at 7PM, room 154 Light Engineering.

Join our mailing list so you can be up-to-date with our upcoming events and opportunities! All our programs are open to all students, regardless of major or degree.

National Membership

Apply online to become a SHPE national member and register with our chapter. Yearly fees are $10 but they come with some neat benefits!

Internships

SHPE hosts a National Conference that takes place once a year that attract hundreds of employers from all different industries looking to hire for internships, co-ops, full time positions, and graduate programs.

Scholarships

Members qualify for Undergraduate and Graduate scholarship opportunities ranging from $1,000 to $5,000.

Expanding your Network

SHPE also offers members several events throughout the year that provide oppurtunities to meet and connect with other SHPE members across the country, from both graduate and undergraduate students to profesional members and corperate leaders.

Leadership Opportunities

As a member of SHPE, you can become a leader at the university level, Regional level, and National level. The National Institute for Leadership Advancement (NILA) is exclusively designed to provide newly-elected student and professional chapter executive board members with the resources for leadership and chapter development.

Professional Development

Events such as Executive Leadership Institute (ELI) cultivate an interactive learning environment where current and potential executives are enabled to examine and enhance their leadership and management skills.

Any other?

Discounted registration to SHPE National, Regional & Local events, complimentary subscriptions to SHPE Magazine, and complimentary SHPE stoles upon graduation.
